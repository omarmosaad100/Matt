A show that has been going for five thousand years

By Sarah Atef

Five thousand years ago in the city of Thebes, the first Puppet in history was made for Egyptian children, it was made from wood. It had joints in the hands and legs and it is present now in the Louvre Museum.

Mohamed Galal Al Din wrote an article in Al-Ahram newspaper on March 2021, he presented a historical review of the development of the Puppet and Al Aragoz in Egypt till the inauguration of the Puppet Cairo Theater in the fiftieth of the 20-century located in Al Azbakia. The title of his article was: “Beginning with the Romanian "five fingers". The Puppet Troupe launches “Shater Hassan” at the opera house.” The Egyptian audience, who knew the art form based on the representative scenes of Puppets in the “Aragoz” shows, got to know the style of “puppet show” in its modern form when the Romanian puppet troupe “Tsandrika” came to perform Marionette shows at the end of the fifties: “The Five Fingers” which gained great admiration from The Egyptian public. This was according to Al-Ahram in its issue of January 23, 1959 and that was the beginning of the story of the Egyptian Puppet Art. 1964 witnessed the launch of the first national festival for various puppet arts, in which the Marionette, Black Theatre, Shadow Show, Schools, TV puppeteer, Chokoko Puppet Theater and Juha Puppet Troupe participated.

Another article written by Hamdy Abdeen in “Al Shark Al Awsat” newspaper on the 10th of December 2020 entitled: “Puppet theater… the maker of joy in the lives of Egyptians”A hit in 1960 was “Al Lila al kebira” one of the most famous Puppet shows, written by the great late Poet Salah Jaheen, Marionette designed by Dr. Nagy Shaker, composed by famous music composer Sayed Mekkawy, music by Ali Ismail, and directed by the Artist Salah El-Sakka. The show presents the typical popular “Al Moulad show” and has been presented for 3 consecutive months with great success on the Theater of the Agricultural Industrial Exhibition. It won the first price in the International Bukharest Festival in 1961. Late President Gamal Abdel Nasser and his guest at this time, King Mohammed V of Morocco, attended the show. Later, the president asked Dr. Tharwat Okasha, the minister of culture to build the Puppet Theater in Al-Azbakeya Park, it costed at that time more than one hundred thousand pounds at that time and became the first complex of Puppet theater in the world. Until now, Marionette shows are presented weekly at 6:30 PM on Thursdays and Fridays with ticket prices of 37 Egyptian Pounds.
