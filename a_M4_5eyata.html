Weesa Wassef, a magical piece of art on the edges of Giza

By Mahmoud Abdelnasser

About 40 kilometers from the heart of the capital, specifically in the village of Harrania, affiliated to the Abu Nomros Center in Giza Governorate. More than 70 years ago, the architect and professor of architecture at the Faculty of Fine Arts, Ramses Wissa Wassef, established an arts center specializing in textiles. Where the idea of the center was to gather the children of the village and start teaching them so they could make unique pieces of art. The craft and later became great artists producing beautiful works of art. In this beautiful artistic and architectural mihrab built of mud, there is a room for each artist who works in it, in a suitable atmosphere that helps him to contemplate and create.



The center helped develop the skills of the children of this village. The children of this village have become great artists. Everyone comes from all over the world to see and acquire their beautiful paintings.

This center is a beautiful artistic and architectural masterpiece started by architect Wissa Wassef and completed by his daughter Suzan and of course the village children who are now great artists.
