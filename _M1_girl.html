The ingenious nine-year-old girl

By Mahmoud Abdelnasser

Inside her room, Menna prepares herself with the help of her mother and her teacher at the same time to present his paragraph in the circus program. The “Kawchuck” player who started playing the game three years ago, when she was six years old, why not, her mother is also a retired Kawchuck player, Rabab Al-Masry, who is one of the most important players in the history of National Circus.



Rabab says I started playing when I was only four years old. I was born and raised inside the walls of the circus. My father was a Kawchuck player and coach, and he taught me the skills of the game. I participated in many shows in various theaters of the world, and when I retired from the game, I wished that one of my children would become a Kawchuck player so that my career would continue, and indeed At the age of six, my daughter liked the game, and with the help of my father, she trained and equipped her until she became a professional Kawchuck player at the age of nine years. Many of the audience come to watch specifically to watch the show she presents.



Menna's Mother added "my daughter inherited the game from me, and before me, her grandfather to her mother, and there is hope that the game will not disappear in the future".



She added with great sadness, "The circus player is a great artist who should be given great attention and appreciation here. What saddens me the most is that when I performed shows all over the world, I found great interest and appreciation for the circus artist, and this is the opposite of what is happening here, as I do not feel appreciated here at all."

She concluded her speech by saying that she hopes that her daughter will be better than her and that she and the Kawchuck players will receive more attention and appreciation in the future.
