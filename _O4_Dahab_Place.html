Dahab.. All the diversity you can get

By Omar Mosaad



Dahab is a small Egyptian town on the south east coast of Sinai, located northeast pf Sharm El-Sheikh. Formerly a Bedouin fishing village, Dahab is now considered to be one of Egypt's most treasured diving destinations. Following the Six-Day War, sinai was occupied by the Enemy, and Dahab got its name changed to a Jewish name. Later, Sinai was restored, and the government helped the arrival of many local and international tourism companies, hotel chains, and the establishment of many other ancillary facilities has since made the town resorts a popular destination with tourists.

Dahab is served by the Sharm El Sheikh Airport. Masbat (within Dahab) is a popular diving destination, and there are 50+ dive centers located within Dahab. Most of Dahab's diving spots are shore dives.

In Dahab you will see the greatest diversity you would see in Egypt. People from all cultures, colors, and nationalities, share same vibes, come to Dahab to have fun time. You would rarely see any type of harassment, you would see girls feeling very safe wearing whatever they want and kids playing safely everywhere. The citizens are very nice, non judgmental, and friendly, they wont treat you based on your gender, belief, or color.

Dahab is full of wonderful sites, activities, and restaurants. There are amazing food from all countries. Many restaurants are run by foreigners who live in Dahab permanently. Actually many foreigners do live in Dahab, since it has got both the eastern vibes they are searching for, and the freedom of the west they are used to.
