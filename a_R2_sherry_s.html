A Vintage Taste for Music: Sherry’s Vinyl

By Rawan Elhadary

For those with a love for good music and all things vintage, Sherry’s Vinyl is a hidden gem and a valuable find. Concealed inside a mundane-looking mall opposite to Flamenco Hotel in Zamalek, this store was named after the woman who founded it in 2007.

From English rock to classical Arabic songs, there is no genre that escapes Sherry’s Vinyl. Their collection of music records and tapes spans across all genres imaginable, through different musical eras and time periods, both in Arabic and in English. The most popular genre at Sherry’s is classical Arabic, which happens to be the rarest genre to find in vinyl format.

Despite the obscure location of the store, several musical artists have actually visited Sherry’s, including Jannis Stürtz (co-founder of Habibi Funk), members of Massar Egbari, and various other artists and actors.

Sherry’s evolution and modernization was gradual, starting from its birth in 2007 up until now. It involved everything from updating of the kinds of music available at the store to moving to a new, larger store and incorporating social media in the marketing process and making online shopping an option.

Sherry’s also eventually branched out of the old, vintage music records and started to also search for and display newer, modern vinyl records by the latest stars of nowadays by importing them from different countries.

Sherry’s decision to open this store stemmed from her love for music and for vintage heritage and her desire to protect them from extinction. She collected and sold vinyl records, collectibles and antique items to a limited number of customers. When her small business eventually grew and required her to move to a larger store, her son, Khaled Ali, helped her to decorate and market for the store, including the creation of a brand name, logo, social media presence and creating awareness for the shop. After months of working on improving the store, Khaled and his mother became partners in running the business. After his mother’s passing away, Khaled became the owner of Sherry’s Vinyl.

In Khaled’s opinion, experiencing music through vinyl records gives the listener a more in depth sense of the instruments and vocals used to make a track. He also believes vinyl records are extremely special items in the world of art, partially because they are a way to pay homage to the artists who created the music and to preserve these records to honor them through collecting records, creating art, posters, or decorations out of the vinyl covers. Khaled believes that vinyl records ignite a sense of passion for art and music in people, as the collection and preservation of old heritage is an expression of art and a great way to honor the masterpiece that is music.
