Representation Through the Cinematic Lense

By Heba Magdy

Over the last several decades, cinema has taken a sharp turn. For the better or for the worse? you might ask. Well, that is very much a matter of perspective and opinion. What is clearly unanimous is the fact that there will always be some aspect of humanity that people may see as still underrepresented. This is for many reasons, but the one that resonates now is quite simple.

There aren’t enough people from EVERY group to represent. Meaning that the directors, producers, executives who dominate the industry tend to gravitate towards relativity and comfort. Scorsese’s Italian background seeps through his masculine, violence drenched world of mobsters and schemes. Nolan’s charismatic and complex character shows in his writing, you may need a physicist or philosopher to get through a movie of his. Shahin’s need to attack the stereotypical archetype of how the west sees the east as a third world country devoid of humor and humane relativity is crystal clear in the worlds he shows the viewers.

That is to say, there are many people who want there to be representation and inclusivity, but what we should not neglect when dissecting this trope of cinema is that the story Infront of you is a human one, relatable in a quintessential and non-verbal way. Our empathy binds us to any character, even those with a skewed moral compass. In other words, the person we are seeing on the screen may very well not look like us, speak our language, or adhere to the same codes and beliefs, but we share more on a spiritual level.

People are different, even if they belong to the same creed, ethnicity, geographical location, political affiliations, and background. And so, it is an impossibility to be able to tell everyone’s story. So, for a change, we should not highlight the differences, but instead, applaud how far cinema has come close to inclusivity, even if that road is a long, never-ending one. And with that being said, here is a list of movies that champion diversity and fought for inclusivity and came out on top:

• Don’t Look Up
• Little Women
• When They See Us
• Parasite
• Beautiful Boy
• Capernaum
• Leave No Trace
• The Fits
• Akeelah and the bee
• Hidden Figures
