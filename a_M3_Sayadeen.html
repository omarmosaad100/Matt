Living on the Nile

By Mahmoud Abdelnasser

On the banks of the Nile on the Isle of Gold they live, fish and eat all of it takes place in small wooden boats. Dozens of families who have narrowed their ways in the streets of Cairo and its alleys are hoping that the Nile be their shelter and source of livelihood. Where the Nile represents everything for them, they married and gave birth inside small wooden boats, which they use for housing and fishing. In some boats, you see entire families consisting of a grandfather, grandmother, their sons and grandchildren, and all these people live in that small wooden box on the surface of the Nile.



When you approach any boat, you will find the necessities of living such as food, water, utensils, a stove, and mattresses for sleeping, as well as a TV and a shower to entertain their children who do not know much about life on dry land. Children there spend their day playing and laughing on the surface of the Nile and between its banks.



The residents of these boats live a quiet life away from the hustle and bustle of the capital, but they lack the most basic necessities of a normal life, such as education, health, and a quality life.
