Museum of Art, but this time, feeling a bit different

By Sarah Atef

This scientific museum is considered to be one of the unique in the world regarding its outstanding medical and scientific collection of rare obstetrical and gynecological specimens although most of the Egyptians are unaware of this Egyptian scientific icon. It was first inaugurated in 1932, in Kasr El Aini medical school by Dr Naguib Pasha Mafouz and carrying his name till now. In 2018, it was totally renovated and updated. It plays an important role in the advancement of women’s health and wellbeing through its mission of continuous professional educational and training purpose of doctors, nurses and midwives. Its recent digital platform and venue carries the latest scientific material and technology related to fetal and maternal health. Anyone interested in this field will be able to virtually visit this unique museum.

Dr Naguib Mahfouz offered in 1928, 1350 unique and rare specimens in the Obstetric and Gynecological field, collected from his operations and donated them to the museum of Kasr Al Aini school which was named after him in 1932. During the visit of the president of the Royal College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists Of England, Sir Eardly Holland, in 1945, he described after his tour in the museum that it was: “a remarkable collection” and” a wonderful monument to the name of its founder”.1

Dr Mahfouz who became the first Egyptian professor of Obstetrics and Gynecology explained why be became specialized in this field in his autobiography (The Life of an Egyptian Doctor, Livingstone, 1966) : that after the tragedy of the death of a baby and his mother in a difficult delivery that he was assisting another doctor, he discovered the lack of such specialty in Egypt that why: “ I resolved to do everything in my power to study obstetrics and gynecology and to dedicate my life to help women suffering from difficult labour.”2
