I am the Clown! The story of Clown Kajo

By Mahmoud Abdelnasser

The idea of presenting the clown’s segment was never in Hassan Kamal’s mind. Hasan, who was born and raised within the walls of the National Circus, started his life as a child in the late seventies as a “biscicleta” player like his father, who taught him the skills of the game until he mastered it and became one of the most famous Biscicleta players in Egypt. He even participated with his colleagues in the National Circus in some international competitions in France, and won the first prize in a circus competition in North Korea, but with good age, as he reached his fifties and reduced his fitness for the beginning and because of his overwhelming love for the circus, Kamal decided to start a new stage of his career within the walls of the circus itself, so he decided to present the clown’s segment, and because of his great experience in circus arts, Hassan was able to excel in presenting this segment and was known as the director “Kajo” and became a source of attraction for the audience, especially children, due to his lightness and heart-loving presence.

Fearing the extinction of the circus art, for which he spent his entire life, his son, a student in the third year of the Faculty of Social Work, taught the skills of the game of piscullet to complete his father’s journey before him, his grandfather.
