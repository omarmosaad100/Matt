Wooden Art craft

By Sarah Atef

Since Ancient Egypt, different handmade art crafts were created by skillful. Wooden sculptures, furniture manufacturing and carpentry were among the earliest beautiful crafts present in ancient in Egypt. This was discovered, preserved with its beautiful color in various tombs demonstrating the advanced wood work crafts men.

In Al-Ahram newspaper Nevine El-Aref wrote an article entitled: “Tutankhmun’s chair is safe and sound, says museum official on the 26 of March 2015.” In this article, she describes how that the carpentry tools that were used in ancient Egypt are described and represented in gravure on the walls of various tombs dating back to the old and middle kingdom. All that proves that ancient Egyptians had skilled laborers in carpentry. The most famous chair is king Tutankhamun among his golden thrown covered with gold leafing.

By time all hand crafts have been substituted by the modern machinery and special handmade art crafts became rare and very expensive. I am going to heighten on NGO non profitable organization working with the youth in upper Egypt in order to develop hand craft skills and education. The Association of upper Egypt for Education and Development (AUEED) has been created in 1940 by father Henry Ayout in order to lead education and development initiative in four main cities of Upper Egypt: Menya, Asyut, Sohag and Luxor. So, this association succeeded to create skilled artisans from upper Egypt. It holds an annual exhibition at its headquartering in Daher. This exhibition shows the wooden hand craft of Hagaza, the most famous Egyptian village for creating wooden hand crafts in Qena. The exhibition is a good opportunity for hand made wooden lovers to get familiar with the Egyptian heritage beauty and culture of ancient Egypt and also encourage to buy these masterpieces at convenience price.
