Caricature, the lost laughing art

By Sarah Atef

The word “Caricature” comes from the Italian word “cairicatare” which means “exaggeration”. There is no doubt that caricature presented in the newspapers, plays a crucial and political role when tackling social and economic problems of the society.

Caricature drawings presented in pharaonic gravures of many temples like that present in Hapo temple, while the beginning of caricature art emerged in ancient Greece. In France, the first “Caricature” newspaper was published in 1832 followed by the “Punch” in Britain in 1833. In Egypt, Rose Al-youssef magazine played a positive role in 1925 in developing the art of caricature in Egyptian newspapers; the artist Saroukan invented the character of “Al Masery Effendi” (Mr. Egyptian) that enabled him to find a connection between the reality and the problems of the Egyptian society at that time.1 Salah Jaheen was a talented cartoonist in Sabah El Kheir magazine in 1950s and 60s that created the character of “Abou Al-Mafhomia” (Mr. Knows Everything), then later by joining Al-Ahram in 1970 and turning towards the political cartoons. An exhibition of his cartoons was held lately in Al-Sehimi house in Al-Hussein district.2 In an article entitiled “Ahmed Ragab (1928 – 2014).. Mustafa Hussein (1935 – 2014).. The pulse of a nation documented with humor” in Al-Watany newspaper in September 2014 described the brilliant collaboration that created vivid represented cartoon characters presented every Saturday in Akhbar Al-Youm newspaper, and that were impressive like “Kambora” the nouveau riche that dreamed to be a parliament member, “Falah Kafr al-Hanadwa” who represents a wisdom paysan who informs the prime minister about the sentiments of the public.3

Nowadays, with the emerging of the internet and media platforms, the role of traditional media, like newspaper has sharply declined all-over the world. Actually, the only Egyptian newspaper presenting caricature on daily basis is Al Masry Al-Youm by the freelancer cartoonist Amr Eissa but with much less attractive than the latter named legends.

I succeeded to visit the Egyptian Caricature Association which opens once weekly and where I found treasure of original caricature drawings with the famous Egyptian Caricaturist handwritings that I am presenting with proud in this article.
