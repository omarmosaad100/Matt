Geziret El Dahab: A couple of kilometers away from heaven

By Omar Mosaad

A couple of kilometers away from Maadi, one of Egypt's most famous neighborhoods, known for being home to rich people, beautiful buildings, and fancy restaurants, we see Geziret El Dahab. It's an island that has no streets, no restaurants, and even not a single Koshk. Its residents live either in red- stone buildings or in boats! The residents of the island either are farmers, and in this case live in cheap buildings, or they are fishermen, and in this case living in boats near the island's coasts. The island has no access to the outer world, no bridge, no internet, and no telephones, no electricity, nothing but a boat taking people to the other side. If one wants to buy a can of soda, the nearest thing is taking the boat to the other side, but if one wants to buy something urgently at night, he or she should wait till morning.

Those fishermen who live in boats, park their boats so close to each other that you can see and hear everything happening in the next boat, there is no way to have some privacy. For more information about the fishermen in Gizeret El Dahab, click here!

The people of the island are very friendly, but it's so sad that they don't have anything to offer as a gesture, they can't invite you to a home since they got none, and if they did it's most probably very crowded. They can't offer you a cup of tea in any cafe or Qahwa since there is none.



During our journey, we encountered many residents, who weren't really interested in letting us highlight them, take photos of their lives, and help them improve it. Simply, because they believe that everyone who's visited them, just took some photos for her/his own benefit and left. Now, what difference can we make?
