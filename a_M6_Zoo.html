Zoo: Putting animals in cages for visitors to enjoy their day

Pictures by Mahmoud Abdelnasser, written by Omar Mosaad

Is it time to question the morality of some of our actions? For ages our ancestors used to dominate animals in all ways possible, including using them for entertainment. But now, when humanity nearly ended most wars, hunger, and plagues, we have time to think about some moral obligation toward those who share the planet with us.



Imagine someone taking your kid to a human museum somewhere in the galaxy because an alien race thinks we are cute, sounds like a horror movie right? Aren't we doing the same here? Not because we have been used to this act for years, means it's right.

We need to consider few things, do animals have a better quality of life in these zoos? Do they get fed well? Do they practice their life freely? Would they go if we open these cages and let them loose? Actually in some countries, animals are much better treated in zoos than they would be in a forest, so animals would never choose going back. But sadly, in Egypt, we would sometimes see animals trying to run away from some zoos.



The thing is, animals health in these zoos is depending wether people will visit and pay or not. Also, the owners will take a share of the revenue and leave the animals a little. These animals not only need to be fed, but social life is important, like having kids and communicating with each other. They also want to do what they have evolved to do, like running, climbing, and playing.
